# My NYC Top 3's: A Local's Guide to the Best Eats in the City

**After countless meals exploring New York's neighborhoods, here are my personal top 3 spots in seven categories — from old-school delis to Korean BBQ.**

---

## Bagels

### 1. Barney Greengrass — Upper West Side

![Barney Greengrass](images/nyc%20top%203s/Restaurant%20pics/Barney%20Greengrass.jpg)

Old school deli around since 1908 that specializes in smoked fish and handles all transactions with cash and paper receipts.

**Get this:** Sturgeon sandwich

### 2. Tompkins Square Bagels — East Village

![Tompkins Square Bagels](images/nyc%20top%203s/Restaurant%20pics/Thompkins%20Square%20Bagles.jpg)

A New York City staple — stand in line and watch the bagels get made before your eyes.

**Get this:** Sausage egg and cheese on an everything bagel with a dollop of cream cheese

### 3. Popup Bagels — Various Locations

![Popup Bagels](images/nyc%20top%203s/Restaurant%20pics/Popup%20Bagles.jpg)

A different take on bagels — buy a 3-pack, rip, and dip into a tub of schmear.

**Get this:** 3-pack with scallion cream cheese schmear

---

## Burgers

### 1. Au Cheval — Tribeca

![Au Cheval](images/nyc%20top%203s/Restaurant%20pics/Au%20Cheval.jpg)

Swanky spot tucked away in an alley serving the same burger as the famous 4 Charles.

**Get this:** Double Cheeseburger with bacon and egg

### 2. Lovely's Old Fashioned — Hell's Kitchen

![Lovely's Old Fashioned](images/nyc%20top%203s/Restaurant%20pics/Lovely's.jpg)

1950s-style burger joint; sit at the counter and watch them make your smashburger with caramelized onions on a potato bun.

**Get this:** Charlotte's Special

### 3. Nowon — East Village

![Nowon](images/nyc%20top%203s/Restaurant%20pics/Nowon.jpg)

Korean-American spot with walk-in seats at the bar and hip hop music.

**Get this:** The Legendary Cheeseburger with bacon and egg

---

## Pizza

### 1. Rubirosa — Nolita

![Rubirosa](images/nyc%20top%203s/Restaurant%20pics/Rubirosa.jpg)

Big pies in a sit-down restaurant with old school Italian-American vibes.

**Get this:** Honey Pie

### 2. Prince St. Pizza — Nolita

![Prince St. Pizza](images/nyc%20top%203s/Restaurant%20pics/Prince%20St.jpg)

Sicilian-style (thick, doughy square slices) known to include vodka sauce.

**Get this:** The Naughty Pie

### 3. L'Industrie Pizzeria — Williamsburg

![L'Industrie Pizzeria](images/nyc%20top%203s/Restaurant%20pics/L'Industrie.jpg.png)

Big, thin-crust slices with burrata / ricotta cheese on top. Order ahead to avoid the line.

**Get this:** New Yorker

---

## Sandwiches

### 1. Carnitas Ramirez — East Village

![Carnitas Ramirez](images/nyc%20top%203s/Restaurant%20pics/Carnitas%20Ramirez.jpg.png)

Beloved spot for tacos that serves a torta (sandwich on a soft roll) as part of a $15 lunch special available Wednesday through Friday, 1-5pm: pork sandwich, chicharrones (fried pork rinds) and a drink. Incredible.

**Get this:** Torta de Carnitas Lunch Special

### 2. Faicco's Italian Specialties — West Village

![Faicco's Italian Specialties](images/nyc%20top%203s/Restaurant%20pics/Faicco's.jpg)

Italian deli selling massive Italian sandwiches loaded with meat. Consider saving half for dinner.

**Get this:** Italian Special

### 3. Danny & Coop's Cheesesteaks — East Village

![Danny & Coop's Cheesesteaks](images/nyc%20top%203s/Restaurant%20pics/Danny%20and%20Coops.jpg)

Serving the best Philly cheesesteaks in New York, Danny & Coop's comes from the same people behind the famous Angelo's in Philadelphia, plus Bradley Cooper.

**Get this:** Cheesesteak Sandwich with a side of sweet peppers

---

## Thai

### 1. Valla Table — Hell's Kitchen

![Valla Table](images/nyc%20top%203s/Restaurant%20pics/Valla%20Table.jpg)

Small spot that serves some of the best curry I've had. Good for small groups or dates.

**Get this:** Massaman Nong

### 2. Sappe — West Village

![Sappe](images/nyc%20top%203s/Restaurant%20pics/Sappe.jpg)

Good food and drinks with a fun 1950s vibe, good for group dinners.

**Get this:** Crying Tiger

### 3. Thai Diner — Nolita

![Thai Diner](images/nyc%20top%203s/Restaurant%20pics/Thai%20Diner.jpg)

Crossover between Thai and diner food (like crinkle fries with curry and peanuts on top). Not many reservations, so put your name down in-person ahead of time.

**Get this:** Panang Neuh

---

## Korean

### 1. Kisa — Lower East Side

![Kisa](images/nyc%20top%203s/Restaurant%20pics/Kisa.jpg)

Korean diner experience based on a kisa sikdang, which serves taxi drivers. Set meal served on a big round tray with your choice for the main and a plethora of side dishes. No reservations, so put your name down and head two doors down to Two Doors Down for a drink while you wait.

**Get this:** Platter with Bulgogi

### 2. Thursday Kitchen — East Village

![Thursday Kitchen](images/nyc%20top%203s/Restaurant%20pics/Thursday%20Kitchen.jpg)

Quirky small plates like truffle oil edamame dumplings and tuna tartar tacos, with hip hop vibes in a cozy space. Great for group dinners — put your name down and head to Romeos while you wait.

**Get this:** Steak, lucky noodle, edamame dumpling

### 3. Gan-Hoo Barbeque — Koreatown

![Gan-Hoo Barbeque](images/nyc%20top%203s/Restaurant%20pics/Gan-Hoo%20BBQ.jpg)

Korean barbeque — grill your own meat on a little gas grill at the table. Go with some friends and avoid overcooking the wagyu.

**Get this:** Meat Lover's Stair Combo

---

## Sushi

### 1. Omakase by Korami — Hell's Kitchen

![Omakase by Korami](images/nyc%20top%203s/Restaurant%20pics/Omakase%20by%20Korami.jpg)

Intimate spot for omakase-style sushi: sit at the bar and watch the chefs make 15 different pieces for you one at a time.

**Get this:** Omakase

### 2. Sugarfish — Multiple Locations

![Sugarfish](images/nyc%20top%203s/Restaurant%20pics/Sugarfish.jpg)

Traditional sushi with a focus on high-quality ingredients.

**Get this:** Trust Me

### 3. Sushi Hatsune — Multiple Locations

![Sushi Hatsune](images/nyc%20top%203s/Restaurant%20pics/Sushi%20Hatsune.jpg)

Best delivery sushi in the city.

**Get this:** Tuna Lovers

---

*Living in NYC means endless options — these are the spots I keep coming back to. Got a recommendation I'm missing? Let me know.*
